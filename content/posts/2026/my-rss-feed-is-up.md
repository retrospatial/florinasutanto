When I set up this blog I knew that I wanted to create an RSS feed for it. RSS continues to be my favorite method of curating what I read on the internet, stripped free of algorithms that control what I can and can't see.

These days, I primarily write and read blog posts on [Dreamwidth](https://www.dreamwidth.org/), a blogging platform that also acts as as feed reader, but adding external, non-Dreamwidth sites there is a little finnicky and I'd rather not [generate accounts for other people's feeds](https://www.dreamwidth.org/support/faqbrowse?faqid=27). So in order for me to subscribe to blogs on indie sites, I've set up NetNewsWire as my reader—it's free and open source, which I always appreciate. I've installed [version 6](https://netnewswire.com/help/mac/6.0/en/getting-started.html) instead of the newest 7, since the latter requires me to upgrade my MacOS to Tahoe (v26) and I want to avoid the Liquid Glass updates as long as I can.

So far, the XML for each of my posts only displays its title, publication date, description, and a link to the full post on my blog. The reason for this is that I anticipate wanting to attach interactive components (graphics, scrollytelling, etc.) to certain posts, but I have no idea how that might show up in the reader. If I end up predominantly publishing static (text- and image-based) posts, I'll probably modify the XML to display the entire post.

You can find my feed [here](/rss.xml).
